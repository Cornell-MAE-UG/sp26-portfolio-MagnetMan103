## About This Project

This project was for my Mechatronics class (MAE3780) where we participated in a robotic cube collecting competition. Our robot, named Monarch, successfully reached the semifinals of the competition. The robot was equipped with a single color sensor and two QTI sensors on a custom mount, utilizing two motors for mobility and two servos for its arms. Our strategy focused on maximizing the collection zone with dual side arms and using a central "attack arm" to disrupt opponents. Developing Monarch was a great experience in integrating mechanical design with sensors and autonomous control strategies.

## My Contributions
I was responsible for the main design of the robot, deploying wide wings to maximize the cube collecting area. 
For the servo arms that extended to collect cubes, I considered two designs. The first design rotated the entire arms outward to get a wide cube collecting space. However, this design was awkward because the rules specify that the robot starts in an 8”x8” square and can expand to a 12” diameter cylinder, but only an 8.49”x8.49” square can be inscribed in a cylinder. This meant that having rectangular arms provided no significant advantage when dropped this way. I was also concerned about having micro servos as the only attachment point for these huge arms. If an enemy robot slammed into them, the servos’ internal gearing could easily be damaged.

For the final design, I split the arm into a base area and an extension arm. The base area optimized the starting 8”x8” square space, while the expander area extended the arms at a 20-degree angle. This allowed the arms to act as a wider funnel while staying within the 12” diameter cylinder limit. The extender arms also rested on the base arm, meaning that the servos did not have to work against gravity, and the servos had a smaller load because they were only carrying an extender instead of the entire arm. Designing this in CAD allowed me to optimize all of these geometry constraints and thoroughly test the mechanisms before committing to manufacturing. This arm design was a major reason for our success, as it allowed our robot to consistently gather cubes during the competition.
